The Indomitable Jaguar

The beautiful, savage jaguar-the Mayan and Aztec god. Yes, itʼs true.The jaguar has been far less frequently featured as a performer, compared to the other Big Cats. This has mostly to do with their personality. A jaguarʼs personality is different from the others. It differs in many ways, all of which contribute to its refusal to be “tamed”.

They are different

Jaguars kill differently. Nearly all mammal predators kill in the same way. They go for the neck, either in back or the throat in front. Jaguars attack the head. They have been often known to rip the cranial cap right off their prey. They have the strongest jaw strength of all the cats, by far. Twice that of a lion. They can open their jaws further than any other cat. They sink their teeth into the skull. This indicates less intimidation. They donʼt worry about getting right in the face of their victims, literally. This lack of intimidation plays a part in the elevated danger of trying to interact with a jaguar you are trying to tame.

Most unpredictable

They are the most unpredictable of all the cats. You simply canʼt get into the head of a jaguar. They are less emotive. Have fewer signals of what they may do. Without notice, they will lunge for you even through a fence.

Moodiest of the cats

They are the moodiest of the cats. In captivity, they most often brood, rather than be openly aggressive. They donʼt react to stimuli in the same way that the other cats do. This is another aspect of their unpredictability.

Very few performing jaguars

There are fewer performing jaguars, therefor there are fewer people that have any knowledge of the special pains, different methods, and extra level of bravery (i.e. cojones) needed to train jaguars. The lion, tiger and leopard live in areas where the capture and training of these animals has gone on for centuries. Therefore there is more knowledge in their taking and their handling.

Feared and worshiped

Jaguars were either feared and killed or feared and worshiped. They just donʼt “break”. They arenʼt necessarily the most dangerous of the cats, for the simple reason that they will avoid you at all costs; more so than the other cats; with the caveat that you leave them alone. If you break that unspoken contract, they will break you before you break them.
